If you own or manage a yoga studio, a sports court, a martial arts academy, or a co-working space in Pakistan, you know the pattern: peak hours are packed, off-peak hours are empty, and you're leaving significant revenue on the table every week.

The solution isn't more marketing. It's a better structure for how your space generates income.

---

## The Three Revenue Streams Every Active Space Should Run

A well-structured active venue doesn't rely on a single income model. It runs three in parallel:

**Stream 1: Programmes** — recurring classes, workshops, or courses at fixed times  
**Stream 2: Slot Bookings** — hourly or daily open bookings for individuals or groups  
**Stream 3: Private Hire Enquiries** — custom full-space bookings for events, shoots, or corporate hire

Most venues in Pakistan run only one of these, or none of them with any structure. Adding the other two doesn't require more staff — it requires better systems.

---

## Stream 1: Programmes

Programmes are your scheduled, recurring sessions. A yoga studio's Tuesday morning Hatha class. A boxing gym's Saturday sparring session. A music studio's weekly ensemble rehearsal slot.

**Why they work:**
Programmes create predictable revenue. A 10-person class at PKR 1,500 per person is PKR 15,000 per session. Twice a week, that's PKR 120,000 a month from a single time slot. Predictable, repeatable, and buildable — good students bring friends.

**What to define for each programme:**
- Name, description, level (beginner / open / advanced)
- Instructor or facilitator
- Day and time (recurring)
- Session duration
- Maximum capacity
- Price per session (or package options — 4-session, 8-session, monthly)
- Prerequisites (if any)

Once defined, a programme listing should be live and bookable within the day. Guests register, pay, and receive their QR pass in the TIKKIT X app. You scan at the door.

**What kills programme revenue:**
Irregular scheduling. If your Tuesday class runs one week and doesn't the next because the instructor was unavailable, you lose trust. Guest retention in programmes is built on consistency. Cancel sparingly and always with advance notice.

## Stream 2: Slot Bookings

Slot bookings are open-access bookings — a guest picks a date, picks an available time, books and pays. No staff required until they arrive.

**Where this works best:**
- Sports courts (tennis, padel, squash, basketball)
- Martial arts mats for open training
- Rehearsal studios booked by the hour
- Co-working desks booked by the day
- Photography studios booked by the half-day

**The model:**
You define your available hours. The system shows guests what's open. They book, pay, and receive confirmation. If someone else has already taken that slot, they see it as unavailable. No phone calls. No back-and-forth WhatsApp messages. No double bookings.

**Revenue example (tennis court, Karachi):**
At PKR 3,500 per hour, a single court running 6 hours of booked sessions per day generates PKR 21,000. Six days a week: PKR 126,000. If you have two courts and can push average utilisation to 70%, that's PKR 441,000 per month — from a system that requires no manual intervention per booking.

**What kills slot booking revenue:**
Making it hard to book. If guests have to send a WhatsApp to check availability, they'll sometimes do it and sometimes give up. The friction of the booking process is inversely correlated with conversion. A direct online booking flow where they can see availability, pick a slot, and confirm immediately converts at a dramatically higher rate.

## Stream 3: Private Hire Enquiries

Private hire is full-space, custom-duration booking — a corporate team offsite, a brand photo shoot, a birthday party, a private yoga session for a group.

Unlike programmes (fixed schedule) and slot bookings (open availability), private hire is bespoke. Every enquiry is different. Which is why it doesn't need a rigid booking flow — it needs a structured enquiry process.

**What to capture in a private hire enquiry:**
- Date and time requested
- Type of event or use
- Number of people
- Duration required
- Any special requirements (AV, catering, décor, instructor)
- Contact name and number

With this information, you can respond with a tailored quote and timeline. An enquiry that comes in structured means you can turnaround a quote in an hour rather than spending 20 minutes clarifying what they actually want.

**Pricing private hire:**
Don't publish private hire rates publicly. The rate depends on date, duration, configuration, and any add-ons. Give a starting-from rate if you want to filter out very low-budget enquiries ("Private hire from PKR 25,000 for a half-day").

**Revenue ceiling:**
Private hire is often the highest per-session revenue. A corporate team booking your space for a full day might pay 5x your regular slot rate. The constraint is that it displaces your regular programmes and open slots, so be deliberate about when you accept full-day private bookings.

## FAQ

**How do I price my slots without underselling?**
Start by calculating your fixed cost per hour of operating the space (rent, utilities, staff). Price your slots at a minimum of 1.5x this — you need margin for marketing, maintenance, and the hours you can't fill. Survey what comparable venues in your city charge. Price at or slightly above market if your facility is clean, well-equipped, and easy to book.

**What if my space is too small to run all three?**
You don't have to run all three simultaneously. A small yoga studio might only run programmes. A bare court might only run slot bookings. Start with whichever model fits your space and capacity, then add a second stream once the first is stable.

**How do I handle cancellations?**
Set and publish a cancellation policy and enforce it consistently. A 24-hour cancellation window with a full refund is standard. Inside 24 hours, a 50% refund or credit toward a future session is reasonable. "No refunds under any circumstances" creates disputes and bad reviews.

**Should I offer membership pricing?**
Membership pricing (a monthly flat fee for unlimited or limited access) works well for high-frequency users — the regular Tuesday-Thursday morning yoga attendee. It reduces churn, improves cash flow predictability, and builds community. It's worth building into your programme pricing as a tier alongside pay-per-session.
